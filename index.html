ABOUT

The “99 Columbians” bulletin space outside Roone A. Auditorium put up in Spring 2010 greeted the current 2014 seniors back when we were doe-eyed freshmen. Four years later, the exhibit hasn’t changed. The original project was initiated to highlight the incredible diversity of student life on campus, but the images taken in 2010 require a much-needed update.

In conjunction with Humans of Columbia University, Ayushi Roy and Wangari Mungai (CC ‘14) are updating the space to better reflect our new memories and friendships as a campus community. HOCU will introduce a whole new portfolio of pictures for the exhibit wall in April 2014 and, borrowing from the Humans of New York model, include quotations from those photographed.

As such, we are looking for stellar photographs of students and other members that contribute daily to the Columbia community (Public Safety officers, Halal cart vendors, Dining Hall staff etc.). We will accept a mix of high quality headshots and full-body shots. We’d also love to work with student designers/artists/coders/photographers who, like us, are enthusiastic about giving back to the Columbia community. If you’ve come all the way to read this page, then we’re sure that you are likely one of these people, and would like to invite you to join us for this project. So lookout for all the beautiful, unique, quirky, humorous, and powerful Kodak moments and send any pictures our way before Monday, March 17!

If none of the above roles describe you, but you’re still interested in HOCU, shoot us an email at humansofcolumbia[at]gmail.com.

HOW TO SUBMIT

DEADLINE: Monday, March 17, 2014

GUIDELINES: No more than two people may be pictured in one photograph. Photos will not be edited by the Humans of Columbia team after submission. The photographer must obtain consent from the subject(s) prior to submitting the photograph for review.

Each photograph will be accompanied by a quotation. The quotations can be something the pictures person said on their own or in response to a question. For inspiration, here are some sample questions:

  • What is your spirit animal?
  • What is the first memory you have about Columbia?
  • What will you remember 20 years after you’ve graduated from Columbia?
  • If you ever had a wet dream about Columbia what would it be?
  • What was your happiest moment on campus?
  • If you could give one piece of advice to a group of fellow Columbians, what would it be?
  • If you could launch one start-up for the Columbia community, what would it be for?
  • Do you remember the time you felt most afraid?
  • What would you never be caught dead doing/wearing?
  • When’s the last time you cried?
  • Do you remember the happiest moment of your life?
  • What’s your favorite part of being a Columbia student?
  • What’s the most poetic thing about living in New York City?

SUBMISSION: Email JPEG (4MB to 8MB) to humansofcolumbia[at]gmail.com with the following information.

  • Photographer Name, UNI, School/Year (if applicable)
  • Subject(s) Name, UNI, School/Year (if applicable) Note: Be sure that subject has given consent for the photo submission.
  • Quotation to accompany the photograph.
